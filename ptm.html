Konni

Konni, a forested village in the State of Kerala in South India, situated on the bank of the mighty Achencoil River has a long and rich history of man-forest interface. Vast expanse of forests of Western Ghats in the background is crisscrossed by numerous streams and rivulets, which add to the exceptional scenic beauty of the landscape.

Perumthenaruvi waterfalls

The Perunthenaruvi waterfalls on the banks of the Pamba river about 10 km from Erumely is a favourite place for both domestic and foreign tourists in Pathanamthitta district, Kerala. This is a natural fall, down a rocky path into a ravine about 100 feet deep. Ideal for an outing, this place is a popular picnic spot. It is accessible by road.

Achankovilar

The Achankovil River is mainly flowing through Pathanmthitta district and there are several townships on the banks of the river till it joins Pampa river. We can see the river flowing through the forest, several churches and temples on the banks,bridges on several points etc when we travel through Pathanamthitta and bear by districts. During monsoon the river overflows in few areas and last month while we were at Konni, the water level was optimum since it was raining all days. Except in few points people take bath in the river.
